<p><strong>The Active Hydroponics Systems Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Hydroponic Drip Systems</li><li>Flood & Drain Systems</li><li>N.F.T. (Nutrient Film Technique)</li><li>Water Culture Hydroponic Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Active hydroponics systems refer to the cultivation of plants without soil, using a nutrient-rich water solution. There are several types of active hydroponics systems available in the market. Hydroponic drip systems involve regularly dripping a nutrient solution onto the plant roots. Flood and drain systems periodically flood the growing medium with nutrient solution and then drain it. Nutrient Film Technique (N.F.T.) systems involve a thin film of nutrient solution continuously flowing over the roots. Water culture hydroponic systems immerse the plant roots directly into the nutrient solution. These systems provide different methods for plants to absorb nutrients and water for optimal growth and yields.</p></p>
