<p><p>The automotive silicone market consists of various types, including High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV), Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV), Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR), Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR), High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR), and Silicone Gels. HTV is heat-cured and provides durability in high-temperature applications. RTV is cured at room temperature and offers flexibility and easy application. LSR is a two-part liquid that provides excellent sealing properties. FSR is resistant to fuel and oil, making it suitable for automotive engine components. HCR is a solid silicone rubber that offers high tear strength and dimensional stability. Silicone gels provide cushioning and shock absorption capabilities in automotive sealing applications.</p></p>
