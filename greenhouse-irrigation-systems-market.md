<p><p>Greenhouse irrigation systems refer to the various methods used to provide water to plants in a controlled environment. The market types include drip irrigation, which involves delivering water directly to the plant roots; micro sprinkler irrigation, where water is sprayed in a fine mist to irrigate plants; sprinkler irrigation, using overhead sprinklers to distribute water evenly; and others, encompassing less common methods like flood irrigation and sub-irrigation. Each type has its advantages and is chosen based on factors such as crop type, greenhouse size, and water availability.</p></p>
