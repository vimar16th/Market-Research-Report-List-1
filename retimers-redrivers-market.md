<p><p>Retimers and redrivers are both used in electronic devices to improve signal integrity and extend the reach of high-speed data transmission. Retimers are specifically designed for longer-distance applications, as they regenerate the incoming signal before transmitting it, ensuring a clean and accurate signal at the output. Redrivers, on the other hand, are used for shorter distances and serve the purpose of amplifying and reshaping the incoming signal, compensating for any loss or degradation that may have occurred during transmission. Both technologies are essential in maintaining reliable and efficient data transfer in various electronic systems.</p></p>
