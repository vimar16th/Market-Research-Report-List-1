The market for these techniques can be divided into different types, namely Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFNs), Transcription Activator-like Effector Nucleases (TALENs), Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats (CRISPR), and Others. ZFNs and TALENs are protein-based methods that can specifically target and edit DNA sequences. CRISPR is a revolutionary technique that utilizes RNA-guided Cas9 enzyme to make precise changes in the genome. The "Others" category includes alternative genome editing techniques that are still under development or not widely adopted yet.</p></p>
