<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy market consists of three main types: cast aluminum alloy, rolled aluminum alloy, and extruded aluminum alloy. Cast aluminum alloy is formed by pouring metal into a mold, then cooling and solidifying it. Rolled aluminum alloy is created by passing the metal through a series of rollers to achieve the desired thickness. Extruded aluminum alloy is produced by forcing the metal through a die to create complex shapes and profiles. These three types of aluminum alloy are commonly used in the automotive industry for various applications due to their lightweight and high-strength properties.</p></p>
