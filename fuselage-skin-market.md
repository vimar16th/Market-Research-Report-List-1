<p><p>The fuselage skin market is categorized into three types based on the shape and curvature of the skin. Single curl skin refers to a fuselage skin with a cylindrical shape, sometimes seen in aircraft wings. Double curvature skin includes surfaces with both convex and concave curves, commonly found in the main body of aircraft. Complex shape skin refers to irregularly shaped surfaces, such as the nose cone of an aircraft. These different types cater to the diverse structural requirements of aircraft manufacturing, offering various options for designers and engineers.</p></p>
