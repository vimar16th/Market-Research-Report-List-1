<p><p>Nuclear imaging systems are used in the medical field to visualize the internal structures and functions of the human body. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) is a type of nuclear imaging system that uses radioactive tracers to detect and map metabolic processes. Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) is another type that uses gamma rays and a rotating camera to create 3D images of organs. Planar scintigraphy systems are a simpler type that use gamma cameras to take 2D images of radiation emitted by injected radioactive substances.</p></p>
