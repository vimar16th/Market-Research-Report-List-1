Body plethysmography monitors changes in the whole body volume, providing insight into overall health and function. Respiratory organ plethysmography, on the other hand, focuses specifically on measuring changes in the lung volume, allowing for the evaluation of respiratory function and detecting any abnormalities. Both types of plethysmography are valuable tools in the field of animal research and can contribute to understanding the physiological changes in animals.</p></p>
