<p><p>Person-to-person (P2P) payments can be classified into two market types: remote payments and proximity payments. </p><p>Remote payments refer to transactions made between individuals who are located in different physical locations. These transactions are typically carried out electronically through online platforms or mobile apps, allowing users to send money to others using their bank accounts or credit/debit cards.</p><p>On the other hand, proximity payments involve transactions between individuals who are physically near each other. This is often facilitated through technologies like Near Field Communication (NFC) or QR codes, enabling users to make direct payments from their mobile devices to another person's device, such as splitting bills at a restaurant or paying a friend for a coffee.</p></p>
