<p><p>The Landfill Gas-to-Energy (LFGE) market includes various types such as thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies involve burning landfill gas to produce heat or electricity. This process involves combustion, gasification, or pyrolysis. Biochemical reactions, on the other hand, use microorganisms to convert the methane gas from landfills into biogas, which can be used for heating, electricity production, or as a transportation fuel. Both methods aim to capture and utilize the methane emitted from landfills, reducing greenhouse gas emissions and generating renewable energy.</p></p>
