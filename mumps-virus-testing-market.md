<p><p>Various types of testing methods are used to detect the presence of mumps virus in patients. These include cell culture/immunofluorescence, semi-quantitative enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA), semi-quantitative chemiluminescent immunoassay, and qualitative real-time polymerase chain reaction (PCR). The cell culture/immunofluorescence method involves growing the virus in a laboratory setting and then staining it with fluorescent antibodies to identify its presence. ELISA and chemiluminescent immunoassay measure the levels of specific antibodies in the patient's blood to indirectly detect the virus. PCR directly amplifies and detects mumps virus DNA in the patient's sample.</p></p>
