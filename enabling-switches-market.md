<p><p>Enabling switches are categorized based on their market types, which include OFF-ON (2-position) and OFF-ON-OFF (3-position). An OFF-ON switch has two positions: OFF, where the switch is in a non-operational state, and ON, where the switch is activated. It functions by allowing or disabling a specific operation. On the other hand, an OFF-ON-OFF switch has three positions: OFF, ON1, and ON2. It enables two different operations, as it can be activated in two different ON states, providing more flexibility in controlling various functions or devices.</p></p>
