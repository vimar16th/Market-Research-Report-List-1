<p><p>Dental anaesthetic needles are classified according to their gauge (G), which refers to the diameter of the needle. The different types include 25 G, 27 G, 30 G, 31 G, and others. The gauge measurement is inversely proportional to the width of the needle, meaning higher gauge numbers indicate narrower needles. For instance, a 25 G needle is wider than a 30 G needle. Dentists choose the appropriate gauge based on the dental procedure, patient comfort, and desired injection speed. These variations in gauge offer flexibility and precision in administering dental anaesthesia.</p></p>
