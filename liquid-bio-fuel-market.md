<p><p>Liquid biofuel refers to an alternative fuel source produced from biomass, and it is classified into two main types: biodiesel and ethanol. Biodiesel is derived from vegetable oils or animal fats through a production process known as transesterification. It can be used as a substitute for diesel fuel in vehicles, reducing carbon emissions. Ethanol, on the other hand, is primarily made from crops like corn or sugarcane through fermentation. It is blended with gasoline as a renewable fuel, improving air quality and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Both biodiesel and ethanol contribute to the shift towards more sustainable transportation options.</p></p>
