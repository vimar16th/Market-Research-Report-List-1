<p><p>The Automotive Keyless Entry System market consists of two main types: Passive Keyless Entry Systems (PKES) and Remote Keyless Entry Systems (RKES). PKES allows the car owner to unlock or lock the vehicle without actively using a key or remote control. It typically utilizes sensors to detect the proximity of the key fob. On the other hand, RKES uses a remote control to lock or unlock the car from a certain distance, often with the push of a button. Both systems provide convenience and security for car owners.</p></p>
