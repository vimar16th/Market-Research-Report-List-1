<p><p>An acousto-optic modulator (AOM) is a device that modulates the intensity, frequency, or phase of a laser beam using sound waves. It consists of a crystal or material that has a controlled variation in its refractive index when subjected to a high-frequency ultrasound wave. This change in refractive index enables efficient modulation of laser beams. AOMs are widely used in various fields such as telecommunications, laser processing, spectroscopy, and imaging applications.</p><p>The global acousto-optic modulator market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The Acousto-Optic Modulator (AOM) market consists of two main types: Fiber-Coupled Acousto-Optic Modulators and Free-Space Acousto-Optic Modulators. In Fiber-Coupled AOMs, the modulator is integrated with an optical fiber, allowing for efficient transmission of the modulated laser beam. This makes them suitable for applications requiring precise beam control in fiber-optic systems. On the other hand, Free-Space AOMs operate with beam propagation in free space, enabling broader laser manipulation capabilities. They are commonly used in applications like laser communication, laser scanning, and spectroscopy.</p></p>
