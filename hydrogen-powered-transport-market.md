<p><p>The hydrogen-powered transport market encompasses various types of fuel cells, including Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs), Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFCs), and other emerging technologies. PEMFCs use a polymer membrane to conduct protons, which enables efficient and compact designs suitable for cars and other light-duty vehicles. PAFCs, on the other hand, employ phosphoric acid as an electrolyte and are better suited for heavy-duty applications like buses and trains. Additionally, there are other promising fuel cell types being developed, which offer unique advantages and applications within the hydrogen-powered transport sector.</p></p>
