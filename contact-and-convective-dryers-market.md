Convective dryers, on the other hand, use a stream of hot air or gas to remove moisture from the material.</p><p>The contact and convective dryers market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4% during the forecast period. The market growth is driven by the increasing demand for efficient drying solutions in various industries, such as food processing, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, and agriculture. Manufacturers are increasingly integrating advanced features, such as smart controls, sensors, and data analytics, into their dryers to improve efficiency, productivity, and energy savings.</p><p>Another trend is the shift towards energy-efficient and environmentally friendly dryers. The rising awareness about energy conservation and environmental sustainability has led to the development of dryers that use renewable energy sources, such as solar and biomass, or incorporate energy-saving technologies, such as heat recovery systems.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing demand for compact and mobile dryers, especially in industries that require onsite or mobile drying solutions. Compact dryers offer flexibility in installation and reduce the overall footprint, while mobile dryers provide portability and convenience for industries with changing drying requirements or multiple locations.</p><p>In conclusion, the contact and convective dryers market is experiencing steady growth, driven by the increasing demand for efficient drying solutions and the adoption of advanced technologies.
