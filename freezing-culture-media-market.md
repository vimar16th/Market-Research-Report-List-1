<p><p>Freezing culture media is used in laboratories to preserve and store living cells and tissues for future use. There are two types of freezing culture media: with DMSO and DMSO-free. DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide) is commonly added to the media as a cryoprotectant to prevent cell damage during freezing. However, some researchers opt for DMSO-free media due to potential toxicity concerns. Both types of media provide a medium for the cells or tissues to be frozen and stored, allowing for their later use in experiments or clinical applications.</p></p>
