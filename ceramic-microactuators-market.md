<p><p>Ceramic microactuators are devices that convert electrical or mechanical energy into small-scale movements. There are two main types of ceramic microactuators: cantilever-type and mobile type. The cantilever-type microactuator uses a flexible ceramic beam that bends when an electric current is applied. This type is commonly used in sensors and microelectromechanical systems (MEMS). On the other hand, the mobile type microactuator consists of ceramic plates that move in a coordinated manner to produce linear or rotational motion. This type is often found in robotics, medical devices, and optical instruments.</p></p>
