<p><p>The OBD2 scanner market is divided into three types: Bluetooth scanners, software-powered scanners, and hand-held scanners. Bluetooth scanners connect to a vehicle's OBD2 port and transmit data to a smartphone or tablet for analysis. Software-powered scanners rely on computer software to read and interpret the diagnostic information from a vehicle. Hand-held scanners are portable devices that directly connect to a vehicle's OBD2 port and display the diagnostic data on their built-in screens. Each type offers convenience and ease of use for diagnosing and monitoring a vehicle's performance.</p></p>
