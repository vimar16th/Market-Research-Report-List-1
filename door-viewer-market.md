<p><p>A door viewer, also known as a peephole, is a small optical device installed on doors that allows people inside to see who is outside without opening the door. It typically consists of a lens and a viewing hole or screen that provides a wide-angle view of the area outside the door. Door viewers are commonly used in residential and commercial buildings as a part of security systems, enabling individuals to identify visitors before granting access.</p><p>The current outlook of the door viewer market is positive and expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. One of the primary factors driving this growth is the increasing concern for security and safety among homeowners and building occupants. As crime rates continue to rise, the demand for door viewers as a basic security measure is expected to increase. Additionally, the growing adoption of advanced security systems and the rise in smart home technology are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>With advancements in technology, many door viewers now feature additional features such as motion detection, night vision, and video recording capabilities. These enhanced functionalities provide users with more comprehensive security solutions, further fueling the demand for door viewers in the market.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has also had a positive impact on the door viewer market. As people spend more time at home and prioritize safety measures, the installation of door viewers has become a popular choice for enhancing home security.</p><p>In conclusion, the door viewer market is expected to grow at a significant rate, with a projected CAGR of 14% during the forecasted period.
