<p><p>The automotive suspension control arm market is divided into two types: upper arms and lower arms. Upper arms are responsible for connecting the upper part of the steering knuckle to the vehicle's chassis, enabling vertical movement and absorbing shocks. On the other hand, lower arms connect the lower part of the knuckle to the chassis, providing stability and allowing horizontal movement. Both types of control arms play a crucial role in maintaining the vehicle's suspension system, ensuring smooth rides, and preventing excessive vibrations.</p></p>
