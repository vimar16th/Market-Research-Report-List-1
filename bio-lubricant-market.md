<p><p>Bio-lubricant market types encompass vegetable oil and animal oil-based lubricants. Vegetable oil-based lubricants are derived from plants such as sunflower, rapeseed, soybean, and palm. They offer advantages like high viscosity index, thermal stability, and better lubricity. Animal oil-based lubricants are extracted from animal fats like tallow and lard. These lubricants are known for their excellent anti-wear properties, high viscosity index, and biodegradability. Both types of bio-lubricants are environmentally friendly alternatives to petroleum-based lubricants, as they are renewable, sustainable, and have lower toxicity levels, reducing harm to ecosystems and ensuring a greener future.</p></p>
