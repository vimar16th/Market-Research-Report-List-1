<p><p>A medical tourniquet is a device used in the field of medicine to apply controlled pressure to a limb or extremity to temporarily occlude the flow of blood. It is primarily utilized during surgical and emergency procedures to aid in the control of bleeding and to create a bloodless field for better visualization.</p><p>The medical tourniquet market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the rising prevalence of chronic diseases that require surgical interventions, an increase in traumatic injuries, and a growing global geriatric population. Additionally, the advancements in medical technology, such as the development of self-locking tourniquets and electronic tourniquet systems, are contributing to the market's expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing demand for portable and disposable tourniquets is also fueling market growth. These products offer convenience, ease of use, and reduced chances of infection transmission, making them highly sought after by healthcare professionals and patients alike.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends that are expected to impact its growth. <p><p>The medical tourniquet market consists of various types, including latex tourniquets and TPE (Thermoplastic Elastomers) tourniquets. Latex tourniquets are made from natural rubber latex and are widely used due to their stretchability and flexibility. However, some individuals may be allergic to latex. On the other hand, TPE tourniquets are made from a combination of plastic and rubber materials, providing similar properties to latex tourniquets but without the risk of allergy. Both types of tourniquets play a crucial role in medical procedures to restrict blood flow and aid in medical procedures.</p></p>
