<p><p>Power equipment testing is categorized into two main types: power primary equipment testing and power secondary equipment testing. </p><p>Power primary equipment testing involves the assessment of essential electrical components like transformers, circuit breakers, and cables, ensuring their reliability and functionality. This testing is crucial in order to detect any potential faults or malfunctions that could lead to power outages or equipment breakdowns.</p><p>Power secondary equipment testing, on the other hand, focuses on auxiliary equipment such as relays, protective devices, and meters. It is conducted to verify their accuracy and performance, guaranteeing the proper monitoring and control of the electrical system.</p><p>Both types of testing are essential to maintain a safe and reliable power infrastructure.</p></p>
