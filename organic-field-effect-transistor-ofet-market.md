<p><p>Organic Field-effect Transistor (OFET) refers to a type of transistor that utilizes organic materials as semiconductors rather than inorganic materials. There are two main types of OFETs: N-type transistors and P-type transistors. N-type transistors are composed of organic materials that have excess mobile electrons, allowing for the flow of negative charges. In contrast, P-type transistors are made of organic materials with a lack of mobile electrons, facilitating the flow of positive charges. Both types play a crucial role in the OFET market for various electronic applications.</p></p>
