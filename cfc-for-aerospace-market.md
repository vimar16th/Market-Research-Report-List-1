Primary Structure CFC refers to the use of CFC materials in the main load-bearing components of the aircraft, such as wings and fuselage. Secondary Structure CFC involves the use of CFC in non-load bearing parts, like fairings and panels. Interior CFC refers to CFC materials used in the cabin interiors for components like seat frames and overhead bins. Engines CFC market involves the application of CFC in engine components, like fan blades and casings. These different market types demonstrate the versatility and widespread use of CFC materials in the aerospace industry.</p></p>
