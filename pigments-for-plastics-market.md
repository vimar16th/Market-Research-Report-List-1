<p><p>Pigments for plastics are categorized into two main types: inorganic pigments and organic pigments. </p><p>Inorganic pigments are typically derived from minerals or metal compounds. They offer excellent heat stability and lightfastness, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Additionally, inorganic pigments provide a wide range of vibrant colors and strong hiding power.</p><p>On the other hand, organic pigments are synthesized from carbon-based compounds. They often offer a broader color spectrum with superior transparency, which makes them popular for transparent and translucent plastic applications. Organic pigments are known for their good dispersibility and are easily processed.</p><p>Both inorganic and organic pigments play a crucial role in enhancing the visual appeal of plastics, providing color options that meet various aesthetic and functional requirements.</p></p>
